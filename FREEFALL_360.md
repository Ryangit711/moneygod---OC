### The Three Hidden Connections

**1. Terminology as Weapon ⇄ Chart Confusion**

Every obfuscated term (TGA, RRP, IOER, SOFR, XCCY basis) that makes plumbing seem impenetrable is the SAME dynamic that makes charts seem random. They're designed to feel complex. The antidote: Feynman breakdown. Reduce every term to its physical meaning. Once you can explain TGA drawdown in one sentence — "the Treasury spending money it already borrowed, injecting reserves" — you've disarmed the weapon. Same for every chart pattern.

```
Plumbing term            →  Feynman one-liner           →  Edge
TGA drawdown            →  Treasury spending reserves   →  Risk-on bias
Repo spike              →  Banks can't borrow           →  Catch the bounce
RRP facility            →  Money market funds parking   →  Liquidity ceiling
Cross-currency basis    →  USD scarcity offshore        →  EUR/USD direction
```

**2. The Daily Schedule IS the System**

Most people in this space read endlessly and trade rarely. The 3-hour schedule is not a productivity hack — it's the EXECUTION LAYER of every file in this repo. All 12,700 lines of plumbing knowledge are useless unless they filter through the bridge into a daily decision. The schedule is where the system becomes alive. Skip the schedule, and the entire repo becomes a library instead of a nervous system.

**3. Fill Your Cup is the Anti-Pattern to Every Trading Failure**

Every blown account story follows the same arc: small win → size up → lose → revenge size → blow. Fill Your Cup inverts this: small win → LOCK the win (Bucket 1) → Prove It phase → Scale It phase → Live It. The first withdrawal is not spending — it's proof that the system works. Without this file, the playbook and eval protocol are just faster ways to blow accounts. With it, they become a compound machine.
