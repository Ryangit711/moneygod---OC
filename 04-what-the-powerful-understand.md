## How Soros-Types Think About the System

### 1. [Money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md) Is Not Real — Belief In It Is Real

The entire system runs on collective belief. The moment enough people stop believing a currency has value, it doesn't. This has happened repeatedly throughout history:

- Weimar Germany (1923) — hyper[inflation](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md) destroyed the mark
- Zimbabwe (2008) — trillion-dollar notes became wallpaper
- Venezuela (2010s) — bolívar collapsed

The people who profit most understand that **confidence is the product**, and they monitor the confidence meter constantly.

### 2. The Map Is Not the Territory

Financial markets are models of reality, not reality itself. When the model diverges too far from reality — as it did with subprime mortgages in 2008 — there is [money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md) to be made by betting against the model. This is exactly what Soros did with the British pound in 1992 (Black Wednesday) and what he and others did in 2008.

### 3. [Debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md) Is the Architecture of Power

At every level of civilization, [debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md) has been used as a tool of control:

| Era | How [Debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md) Enforced Power |
|-----|------------------------|
| Ancient Mesopotamia | [Debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md) peonage — farmers who couldn't pay [debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)s became bound servants |
| Ancient Rome | *Nexum* — [debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md) bondage was so extreme it triggered revolts and reform |
| Medieval Europe | Feudal obligations were [debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md) relationships — labor owed to the lord |
| Colonial era | [Debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)s imposed on colonies forced resource extraction and political subservience |
| Modern IMF/World [Bank](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md) | "Structural adjustment" loans force developing nations to privatize, deregulate, and cut social spending — [debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md) as neocolonialism |

### 4. Crisis Is Opportunity

Those who understand the system don't fear crises — they anticipate them:

- Crises destroy the wealth of the uninformed
- Crises concentrate assets into fewer hands (the ones who can buy at the bottom)
- Crises justify policy changes that would never be accepted in calm times (shock doctrine)
- Crises reward those who bet against the consensus

### 5. Who Writes the Rules Matters More Than Who Plays the Game

The [financial system](05-how-money-flows-in-the-system.md) is not a level playing field. It is a designed system with rules that favor the rule-makers:

- [Central [bank](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md)](05-how-[money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md)-flows-in-the-system.md)s set [interest rate](08-forex-trading-the-purest-flow.md)s — this determines the cost of all [debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)
- Governments define legal tender — this forces everyone into the currency system
- [Bank](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md) regulations determine who can create [money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md) and under what terms
- Tax policy determines who keeps what they earn

These rules are not handed down by nature. They are **political choices** made by people with interests.

## The [Debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md) Jubilee: An Ancient Solution

Ancient societies understood that unpayable [debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md) would eventually consume everything. Their solution: **the [debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md) jubilee** — a periodic cancellation of all [debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)s.

- **Sumer and Babylon** (~2400–1900 BC): Rulers like Enmetena and Hammurabi issued *andurarum* decrees cancelling [debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)s and freeing [debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md) slaves
- **Ancient Israel**: The Sabbath year (every 7th year) and Jubilee year (every 50th year) cancelled [debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)s and restored land to original families
- **Purpose**: Prevent wealth concentration, maintain social stability, keep the household (oikos) viable

These were not acts of charity. They were acts of **political survival**. When too many people fall into [debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md) peonage, the system collapses. The rulers understood this. Modern rulers often do not — or choose to serve the [credit](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md)ors instead of the household.
