## REGION 1: 🇺🇸 US — THE ENGINE
### Dollar is 88% of FX turnover. Everything else is noise.

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Central Bank** | Federal Reserve — most powerful institution on Earth |
| **Money Creation** | Fed creates reserves → banks create broad money via lending |
| **Liquidity Levers** | Fed Funds Rate, QE/QT, TGA balance, RRP facility, Discount Window, FX swap lines |
| **Currency** | USD — global reserve (59% of global FX reserves), 88% of all FX turnover |
| **What Standardizes** | SOFR, Fed Funds, US Treasury yield curve (the world's risk-free curve) |
| **Key Data** | NFP, CPI, PCE, FOMC, TGA, RRP, Fed balance sheet, JOLTS, retail sales, ISM |
| **Markets** | All USD pairs, SPX/NDX/DJ, US Treasuries (ZN, ZB, ZF, 10Y), WTI, Gold |

**Triggers:**
- Fed cuts rates → dollar liquidity injected → everything rallies globally
- Fed hikes rates → dollar liquidity drained → everything sells off
- QT accelerates → TGA rising → reserves draining → risk-off
- TGA drawn down → reserves injected → risk-on
- RRP declining → reserves flowing into markets → bullish
- RRP rising → cash sitting at Fed, not in markets → bearish

**Region-to-Region Transmission:**
```
Fed Action         → USD moves        → Everything else adjusts
                                      
Rate cut / QE      → USD weakens      → EUR/USD ↑, USD/JPY ↑, Gold ↑
                                       → Commodities ↑ (denominated in USD)
                                       → EM currencies rally (debt cheaper)
                                       → AUD, NZD, CAD, INR all gain
                                       → China exports get cheaper (yuan weaker)

Rate hike / QT     → USD strengthens  → EUR/USD ↓, USD/JPY ↓, Gold ↓
                                       → Commodities ↓
                                       → EM currencies sell off (debt expensive)
                                       → China capital outflows (yuan pressure)
                                       → Japan, EU, EM all suffer
```

## REGION 2: 🇨🇳 CHINA — THE FACTORY & CHALLENGER
### Second largest economy. Driver of commodity super-cycles. Challenger to dollar hegemony.

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Central Bank** | People's Bank of China (PBOC) |
| **Money Creation** | PBOC sets rates/RRR → state banks lend to SOEs, real estate, infrastructure |
| **Liquidity Levers** | LPR (loan prime rate), RRR (reserve requirement ratio), MLF (medium-term lending facility), PSL (pledged supplementary lending), yuan fixing |
| **Currency** | CNY/CNH — managed float, band around fixing, capital controls |
| **What It Drives** | Global commodity demand (iron ore, copper, coal, oil, soybeans), global supply chain, EM exports |
| **Key Data** | GDP, Caixin/PMI, industrial production, trade balance, credit growth (social financing), property sales, yuan fixing rate |
| **Key Link** | China demand ↔ Australia (iron ore), Brazil (soy/iron), Chile (copper), Saudi (oil), NZ (dairy), US (Treasury buyer) |

**Triggers:**
- China stimulus (infrastructure, property) → commodity super-cycle → AUD, NZD, CAD, BRL all rally
- China slowdown/property crisis → commodity crash → AUD, NZD, CAD sell off
- PBOC eases (cuts RRR/LPR) → yuan weakens → EM Asia competitive devaluation
- PBOC tightens → yuan strengthens → EM Asia import deflation
- China buys Treasuries → US rates lower → risk-on
- China sells Treasuries → US rates higher → risk-off
- Yuan devaluation (competitive) → EM Asia FX war → global deflationary shock

**Region-to-Region Transmission:**
```
China Action        → Commodity Channel     → FX Impact
                     → Trade Channel         

Stimulus ↑          → Iron ore, copper, coal ↑ → AUD ↑, NZD ↑
                     → WTI, soybeans ↑          → CAD ↑, BRL ↑
                     → EM exports ↑            → EM FX ↑
                     → Yuan stable or up       → USD/CNY ↓

Slowdown ↓          → Iron ore, copper, coal ↓ → AUD ↓, NZD ↓
                     → WTI ↓                    → CAD ↓
                     → EM exports ↓             → EM FX ↓
                     → Yuan fix lower            → USD/CNY ↑

Sanctions / Tariffs → Supply chains shift       → Regional FX realigns
                     → Vietnam/Mexico gain       → TWD, KRW, MXN adjust
                     → China pivots to BRICS     → De-dollarization narrative
```

## REGION 3: 🇪🇺 EUROPEAN UNION — THE REGULATORY & DEMAND HUB
### EUR/USD is the most traded pair. ECB is the second most powerful central bank.

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Central Bank** | European Central Bank (ECB) — manages euro for 20 countries |
| **Money Creation** | ECB creates euros via TLTROs, APP, PEPP → banks lend to EU economy |
| **Liquidity Levers** | Deposit rate, Main Refinancing Rate, TLTRO repayments, APP/PEPP, STR (€STR) |
| **Currency** | EUR — second most held reserve currency (~20% of global reserves) |
| **What It Drives** | EUR/USD (28% of all FX turnover), bund yields (EU risk-free rate), European equity flows |
| **Key Data** | ECB rate decisions, Eurozone PMI, German GDP, ZEW, IFO, EU CPI, TLTRO activity, periphery spreads (Italy, Spain → Bund spread) |
| **Markets** | EUR/USD, GBP/USD, EUR/CHF, Bund (FGBL), BTP (Italian bonds), Euro Stoxx 50 |

**Triggers:**
- ECB more hawkish than Fed → EUR/USD ↑ (EUR strength)
- ECB more dovish than Fed → EUR/USD ↓ (EUR weakness)
- Eurozone crisis (debt, energy, migration) → EUR/USD ↓, peripheral spreads widen
- ECB TLTRO → liquidity injected into EU banks → EUR weak initially, then risk-on
- Russia-Ukraine energy shock → EU inflation ↑ → ECB forced to hike → recession risk

**Sub-Regions:**
```
│ 🇩🇪 Germany — Manufacturing engine. ZEW/IFO = EU barometer. Industrial production ↔ China demand.
│ 🇫🇷 France — Services/Fiscal. Bond market pressure when deficits rise.
│ 🇮🇹 Italy — Periphery risk. Spread vs Bund = EU stress gauge.
│ 🇪🇸 Spain — Tourism/services. Bond spreads follow Italy.
│ 🇳🇱 Netherlands — Trade surplus. Stronger fiscal position.
│ Nordics (SE, NO, DK) — Not euro but EU-adjacent. NOK/SEK follow EUR.
```

**Region-to-Region Transmission:**
```
ECB Action          → EUR/USD moves     → Everything else adjusts
                     → EU rates change  

Hawkish (hike/QT)   → EUR/USD ↑         → USD weaker across board
                     → EUR/CHF ↑         → CHF safe haven flows shift
                     → Bund yields ↑     → EU borrowing costs ↑
                     → Periphery stress  → Italy/Greece spreads widen

Dovish (cut/QE)     → EUR/USD ↓         → USD stronger
                     → EUR/JPY ↓         → EUR carry trades unwind
                     → Bund yields ↓     → EU borrowing costs ↓
                     → Risk-on EU assets → Euro Stoxx ↑
```

## REGION 4: 🇯🇵 JAPAN — THE CARRY TRADE ENGINE
### Last developed economy with negative rates. Largest foreign holder of US Treasuries.

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Central Bank** | Bank of Japan (BOJ) |
| **Money Creation** | BOJ buys JGBs (government bonds), ETFs, and corporate bonds. Massive QE since 2013. |
| **Liquidity Levers** | Policy rate, YCC (yield curve control — cap on 10Y JGB yield), JGB purchases, ETF purchases |
| **Currency** | JPY — the ultimate funding currency for carry trades |
| **What It Drives** | USD/JPY (most traded pair after EUR/USD), yen carry trade (borrow JPY → buy higher-yielding assets globally), JGB yields (global benchmark for low yield) |
| **Key Data** | BOJ rate decisions, CPI, GDP, Tankan survey (business confidence), trade balance, JGB auction, 10Y JGB yield |
| **Critical Fact** | Japan holds ~$1.1T of US Treasuries (largest foreign holder). If Japan sells Treasuries → US rates rise globally. |

**Triggers:**
- BOJ keeps rates low → JPY weakens → USD/JPY ↑ → risk-on (carry trade active)
- BOJ hikes / YCC removed → JPY strengthens → USD/JPY ↓ → carry trade unwinds → global risk-off
- Japan sells Treasuries (to defend JPY) → US 10Y yields ↑ → global rates ↑
- Risk-off globally → JPY strengthens (repatriation of yen carry) → everything sells off
- Japan CPI rises → BOJ forced to normalize → global rates realign

**The Carry Trade Mechanism:**
```
Normal environment:
  Borrow JPY at 0-0.5% → Convert to USD → Buy US Treasuries at 4-5%
  → Risk-free 3-4% carry + any FX appreciation

Risk-off unwind:
  Sell US Treasuries → Convert to JPY → Repay loan
  → USD/JPY drops → More selling → Cascade
```

**Region-to-Region Transmission:**
```
BOJ Action          → JPY moves         → Global carries adjust
                     → JGB yields        → US rates follow

YCC removed/hike    → JPY ↑             → Carry trade unwinds
                     → USD/JPY ↓         → All yen pairs drop
                     → Japan sells T-bills → US 10Y ↑ → Global rates ↑
                     → Risk-off globally → SPX down, EM down, Gold down

Dovish/no change    → JPY ↓ or stable   → Carry trade remains active
                     → USD/JPY ↑ or flat → Risk-on
                     → Japan funds US deficit → US rates lower
                     → Nikkei ↑ → Asian equities follow
```

## REGION 5: 🇬🇧 UK (LONDON) — THE FX CAPITAL
### London is the center of global FX turnover. UK is the financial services hub.

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Central Bank** | Bank of England (BOE) |
| **Money Creation** | BOE sets Bank Rate → creates reserves → UK banks lend |
| **Currency** | GBP — higher volatility, risk proxy, Brexit premium |
| **What It Drives** | GBP/USD (cable), EUR/GBP, London Fix (gold & FX benchmarks), global FX settlement |
| **Key Data** | BOE rate decisions, UK CPI, GDP, unemployment, retail sales, PMIs |
| **Critical Role** | London handles ~38% of global FX turnover. Two-thirds of all FX trades flow through London. |

**Triggers:**
- BOE rate decisions → GBP moves, affects EUR/GBP, GBP/JPY
- UK inflation → BOE forced to act → GBP volatility
- Brexit/geopolitical news → GBP risk premium
- London Fix (3pm/4pm London time) → daily gold benchmark, FX volatility

## REGION 6: 🇨🇭 SWITZERLAND — THE SAFE HAVEN
### Neutrality + banking secrecy + gold. CHF is the ultimate safe-haven currency.

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Central Bank** | Swiss National Bank (SNB) |
| **Money Creation** | SNB sets policy rate, engages in FX intervention (actively manages CHF) |
| **Currency** | CHF — the perpetual safe haven. Strengthens in every crisis. |
| **What It Drives** | USD/CHF, EUR/CHF, gold storage, private banking, global wealth management |
| **Key Data** | SNB rate decisions, FX reserves, Swiss sight deposits, CPI, GDP |
| **Unique Tool** | Negative rates + FX intervention. SNB is the only CB that directly buys foreign stocks. |

**Triggers:**
- Global crisis → CHF strengthens (safe haven) → EUR/CHF ↓, USD/CHF ↓
- SNB intervenes to weaken CHF → EUR/CHF ↑, USD/CHF ↑
- Risk-on → CHF weakens (funding currency, like JPY)

## REGION 7: 🇦🇺 AUSTRALIA — THE CHINA PROXY
### Commodity super-cycle barometer. AUD = liquid risk-on proxy.

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Central Bank** | Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA) |
| **Money Creation** | RBA sets cash rate → creates reserves → Australian banks lend |
| **Currency** | AUD — commodity currency, China proxy, risk-on barometer |
| **What It Drives** | AUD/USD, AUD/JPY (risk-on carry pair), NZD, iron ore, coal |
| **Key Data** | RBA rate decisions, CPI, GDP, employment, trade balance (China exports), iron ore prices |
| **Primary Export** | Iron ore (China's steel mills), coal, LNG, gold, education services |

**Triggers:**
- China stimulus/infrastructure → iron ore ↑ → AUD ↑
- China slowdown → iron ore ↓ → AUD ↓
- RBA hikes → AUD carry ↑ → AUD/JPY rallies (risk-on carry)
- Global risk-off → AUD/USD ↓ (first to be sold)
- Commodity prices ↑ → Australian terms of trade ↑ → AUD ↑

**Relationship:**
```
AUD = Liquid proxy for China + Emerging Markets + Commodities
AUD rallies when: China stimulus, risk-on, commodity super-cycle, Fed dovish
AUD falls when: China slowdown, risk-off, commodity crash, Fed hawkish
```

| **Money Creation** | RBNZ sets OCR (Official Cash Rate) → creates reserves |
| **Currency** | NZD — agricultural commodity currency, often moves with AUD |

| **Key Data** | RBNZ rate decisions, CPI, GDP, employment, dairy auctions (GDT), milk powder prices |

- Dairy prices ↑ (Global Dairy Trade auction) → NZD ↑

- RBNZ rates relative to RBA → AUD/NZD pair

**AUD/NZD Relationship:**
```
AUD/NZD = Relative commodity proxy

  RBA vs RBNZ rate differential → which CB is more hawkish
```

## REGION 9: 🇸🇬 SINGAPORE — THE ASIAN FINANCIAL HUB
### Third-largest FX center after London and New York. MAS manages via FX.

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Central Bank** | Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) — unique: manages via FX rate, not interest rate |
| **Money Creation** | MAS manages SGD NEER (nominal effective exchange rate) band → banks create SGD via lending |
| **Currency** | SGD — trade-dependent, managed float (NEER band), stable and strong |
| **What It Drives** | USD/SGD, Asian FX correlations, wealth management, Asian bond market |
| **Key Data** | MAS policy statement (semi-annual), GDP, CPI, trade balance, retail sales |
| **Role** | Asian financial hub — $4T in assets under management, largest wealth management center in Asia |

**Triggers:**
- MAS tightens (appreciates NEER band) → SGD strengthens → Asian FX follows
- Global risk-off → SGD/USD stable (managed), but Asian FX sells off
- Trade-dependent → global trade volumes directly affect SGD
- Singapore GDP → proxy for Asian trade health

## REGION 10: 🇮🇳 INDIA — THE GROWTH DEMOGRAPHIC
### Third largest economy by PPP. Oil importer. IT exporter. High growth = high inflation.

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Central Bank** | Reserve Bank of India (RBI) |
| **Money Creation** | RBI sets repo rate → creates reserves → Indian banks lend |
| **Currency** | INR — managed float, RBI actively intervenes to prevent volatility |
| **What It Drives** | USD/INR, Indian bond market (growing global inclusion), gold demand (largest gold importer) |
| **Key Data** | RBI rate decisions, CPI, GDP, industrial production, trade deficit, oil prices, monsoon/agriculture |
| **Primary Imports** | Crude oil (80%+ imported), gold, electronics, coal, chemicals |
| **Primary Exports** | IT services, pharma, textiles, gems, engineering goods |

**Triggers:**
- Oil prices ↑ → India's trade deficit widens → INR weakens (RBI defends)
- Oil prices ↓ → India's trade deficit narrows → INR stable/strengthens
- Global IT spending ↑ → India IT exports ↑ → INR support
- RBI rate decision → INR carry (among highest in Asia)
- US rate differential → FII (foreign institutional investment) flows in/out of India
- China+1 / supply chain shift → India FDI ↑ → structural INR support

**Key Relationship:**
```
INR = Inverse oil price proxy
India = Beneficiary of China+1, victim of oil shocks
USD/INR = Sensitive to Fed policy + RBI intervention
```

## REGION 11: 🇷🇺 RUSSIA — THE ENERGY WILDCARD
### Oil, gas, sanctions, BRICS. Affects Europe directly, everyone else via energy prices.

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Central Bank** | Central Bank of Russia (CBR) |
| **Money Creation** | CBR sets key rate → creates reserves → Russian banks lend |
| **Currency** | RUB — managed float with capital controls since 2022 |
| **What It Drives** | USD/RUB, EUR/RUB, global energy prices (oil, gas, uranium), gold (Russia is largest miner), BRICS de-dollarization |
| **Key Data** | CBR rate decisions, oil production, gas flows to Europe, sanctions news, gold reserves |
| **Primary Exports** | Crude oil, natural gas, coal, gold, wheat, fertilizers, uranium, titanium |

**Triggers:**
- Sanctions tightening → RUB sell-off, energy supply disruption → oil ↑, gas ↑ → Europe inflation
- Sanctions easing → RUB rallies, energy supply stabilizes → oil ↓, gas ↓
- Russia cuts gas to Europe → EU energy crisis → EUR weakens, USD strong
- BRICS expansion + de-dollarization → long-term threat to USD dominance
- Russia-China alignment → Eurasia energy axis → shifts global trade flows

**Sanctions Impact:**
```
US/EU sanctions         → Russia oil exports redirected to China/India
                        → Global oil trade routes realigned
                        → Russia uses gold + yuan + BRICS currency
                        → Long-term: fractionalization of global financial system
                        → USD hegemony subtly eroded (but still dominant)
```

## THE FLOW VERDICT (Daily)

```
IF FED IS INJECTING (TGA ↓, RRP high/falling):
    → Risk-on → Long AUD, NZD, CAD
    → Short JPY (carry trade active)
    → Gold, WTI both up

IF FED IS DRAINING (QT, TGA ↑, RRP exhausted):
    → Risk-off → Short AUD, NZD, CAD
    → Long JPY (carry trade unwinds)
    → Long CHF (safe haven)
    → Gold mixed (safe haven vs USD strength)

IF CHINA IS STIMULATING:
    → Long AUD, NZD, CAD
    → Long WTI, copper, iron ore futures

IF CHINA IS SLOWING:
    → Short AUD, NZD, CAD
    → Short WTI, copper, iron ore

IF BOJ IS INTERVENING / HIKING:
    → Whole system reprices
    → Yen strength across all pairs
    → Carry trade unwinds → risk-off globally

IF GEOPOLITICAL CRISIS (Russia, Middle East):
    → Long USD, CHF, Gold
    → Short EUR (if Europe exposed)
    → Short AUD, NZD (risk proxies)
    → WTI through the roof
```
