# ACCUMULATION & DISTRIBUTION SYLLABUS — 12 Weeks to Execution Ready

## "Each cycle: one concept absorbed, one pattern tested against live data."

---

## THE FRAMEWORK

One week = one cycle. Every cycle has three phases:

```
ACCUMULATION (Weekend — 60-90 min)
│  1. Open the week's concept (see curriculum below)
│  2. Read its hierarchy-master Part 9.2 definition + plumbing cross-ref
│  3. Collect 5 chart examples from the past 30 days
│  4. Write: "When [plumbing signal] → look for [ICT pattern] → trade [setup]"
│  5. Set 3 specific entry rules for the week
│
DISTRIBUTION (Trading Days — Mon/Wed/Fri or Tue/Thu)
│  1. Run live-data-workflow → get verdict
│  2. Open gangotri-protocol — check active edges against THIS WEEK'S concept
│  3. If concept's pattern triggers AND passes the 3-question gate → take the trade
│  4. Journal: date, setup, plumbing context, win/loss, R:R, lesson
│  5. Target: 3-4 trades applying THIS WEEK'S concept
│
REVIEW (Friday close — 15 min)
│  1. Count: concept-specific setups seen vs. taken vs. won
│  2. Did the plumbing context help or hurt the pattern?
│  3. Decision: advance to next concept OR repeat this week
│  4. Decision rule: ≥50% win rate over ≥3 trades = advance. Otherwise repeat.
```

## THE 12-WEEK CURRICULUM

### MONTH 1 — FOUNDATION PATTERNS (Weeks 1-4)

**Week 1 — LIQUIDITY SWEEP (PDH/PDL sweeps)**

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **ICT concept** | Sweep of prior day high/low (PDH/PDL). Price breaches the level, takes out stops, reverses. |
| **Plumbing cross-ref** | hierarchy Part 5, Edge 6 (Stop Hunting). The plumbing context: why did the sweep happen? |
| **What to look for** | Price approaches PDH/PDL → breaches by 1-3 ticks → closes back inside the range. |
| **Entry rules** | 1. Wait for the close back inside. 2. Enter on the retest of the breached level. 3. Stop beyond the sweep extreme. |
| **Proof to advance** | 3 trades taken, ≥50% win rate. Otherwise: repeat Week 1. |

**Week 2 — FAIR VALUE GAP (FVG identification + retracement)**

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **ICT concept** | Three-candle gap where the middle candle's wicks leave a space between the first and third candle bodies. |
| **Plumbing cross-ref** | hierarchy Part 5, Edge 11 (Liquidity Void). A thin order book zone — institutional footprint. |
| **What to look for** | On 5-min or 15-min: a clear three-candle imbalance. On 4H: interbank FVG. |
| **Entry rules** | 1. Identify the FVG after displacement. 2. Wait for price to return to FVG edge. 3. Enter on the first rejection candle at the edge. 4. Target: the opposite edge of the FVG (measured move). |
| **Proof to advance** | 3 trades, ≥50% win rate. |

**Week 3 — DISPLACEMENT (Initiative vs. response candles)**

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **ICT concept** | A large directional candle that breaks structure. The institutional initiative. Followed by retracement (response). |
| **Plumbing cross-ref** | hierarchy Part 9.2 (Displacement row). The plumbing source: institutional order flow that needs a counterparty. |
| **What to look for** | A candle with unusually large range on 5-min or 15-min. Volume spike. Breaks a key level. |
| **Entry rules** | 1. Spot the displacement. 2. DO NOT enter on the displacement candle. 3. Wait for the first retracement to the displacement zone. 4. Enter at the FVG edge created by the displacement. |
| **Proof to advance** | 3 trades where you correctly identified displacement BEFORE entry. 50%+ win rate. |

**Week 4 — OPTIMAL TRADE ENTRY (62-79% retracement)**

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **ICT concept** | After a leg, price retraces to 62-79% of that leg before continuing. The Fibonacci zone where institutions place resting orders. |
| **Plumbing cross-ref** | hierarchy Part 9.2 (OTE row). The retracement zone = the institutional order cluster. |
| **What to look for** | A clean leg → retracement into the 62-79% zone → reversal candle at the zone boundary. |
| **Entry rules** | 1. Identify the leg (clear swing point A → B). 2. Mark the 62% and 79% levels. 3. Wait for price to enter the zone. 4. Enter on reversal candle at either boundary. 5. Stop beyond the swing point. |
| **Proof to advance** | 3 entries at OTE zone, track how many respected the zone before continuing. |

### MONTH 2 — TIMING & CONTEXT (Weeks 5-8)

**Week 5 — KILLZONE TIMING (Execution windows)**

| Element | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **ICT concept** | London (2-5am ET), NY am (7-10am ET), NY pm (10am-12pm ET). Each zone has distinct institutional behavior. |
| **Plumbing cross-ref** | hierarchy Part 9.2 (Killzone row). Each zone maps to specific plumbing events: FX fix, MOC, options expiry. |
| **What to look for** | Entries within 30 min of killzone start have higher probability. Entries outside killzones are lower probability. |
| **Entry rules** | 1. Only take entries within killzone windows. 2. If a setup triggers outside a killzone: note it, don't trade it. 3. Use the extra time for analysis, not entry. |
| **Proof to advance** | Every entry this week must be inside a killzone. Track how many setups you SKIPPED because they were outside. |

**Week 6 — ORDER BLOCKS (Institutional supply/demand zones)**

| Element | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **ICT concept** | The last candle before a strong move. The zone where institutions placed their resting orders. |
| **Plumbing cross-ref** | hierarchy Part 1.4 (Cantillon geometry). Where money enters determines who benefits. Order blocks are the footprint of that entry. |
| **What to look for** | On 15-min or 1H: a consolidation candle (small body, long wick) immediately before a large displacement. That consolidation is the order block. |
| **Entry rules** | 1. Identify the displacement. 2. Find the order block (candle BEFORE the displacement). 3. Wait for price to return to the order block zone. 4. Enter on rejection of the order block boundary. |
| **Proof to advance** | 3 entries at order block boundaries. Track: did price respect the zone? |

**Week 7 — CHANGE OF CHARACTER (CHoCH — structure break)**

| Element | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **ICT concept** | Price breaks the current trend structure (lower low in uptrend, higher high in downtrend) and reverses. The trend is no longer valid. |
| **Plumbing cross-ref** | hierarchy Part 9.2 (New high/low and flip row). The paradigm shift — retail trapped, institutions positioned opposite. |
| **What to look for** | Uptrend: price makes a lower low (breaks the sequence of higher lows) → closes back above the prior swing low. |
| **Entry rules** | 1. Identify the current structure (series of higher highs/lows or lower highs/lows). 2. Wait for the structure break. 3. Enter on the close of the candle that confirms the break. 4. Stop at the extreme of the break candle. |
| **Proof to advance** | Identify 5 CHoCH events correctly BEFORE the second candle forms. |

**Week 8 — PREMIUM/DISCOUNT ARRAYS (50% equilibrium trading)**

| Element | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **ICT concept** | The 50% level of any range is the equilibrium. Above = premium (expensive), below = discount (cheap). Institutions buy discount, sell premium. |
| **Plumbing cross-ref** | hierarchy Part 5, Edge 2 (TGA drawdown → equilibrium). The plumbing also seeks equilibrium — TGA drawdown = injection to restore balance. |
| **What to look for** | Daily or 4H range. Price above 50% midpoint → look for sell setups. Price below 50% → look for buy setups. |
| **Entry rules** | 1. Mark the day's range (high to low). 2. Mark the 50% midpoint. 3. If price is above 50%: only take shorts with sellside targets. If below: only take longs with buyside targets. |
| **Proof to advance** | 3 trades from disequilibrium back to equilibrium. |

### MONTH 3 — SYNTHESIS (Weeks 9-12)

**Week 9 — INTERBANK FVG (Higher timeframe institutional footprint)**

| Element | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **ICT concept** | An FVG on the weekly or daily chart that hasn't been filled. These are the highest-probability targets. |
| **Plumbing cross-ref** | hierarchy Part 9.2 (IFC row). Weekly FVGs = institutional positioning, not day-trader noise. |
| **What to look for** | Open weekly chart. Look for any unfilled FVGs from the past 4-8 weeks. Mark them. |
| **Entry rules** | 1. Identify the IFC (weekly FVG). 2. Wait for price to approach it. 3. Enter at the edge of the IFC with a reversal pattern on the 1H chart. 4. Target: the opposite edge (measured move can be hundreds of points). |
| **Proof to advance** | 3 trades targeting interbank FVGs. |

**Week 10 — LIQUIDITY PYRAMID (Multiple timeframe confluence)**

| Element | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **ICT concept** | Price sweeps liquidity on multiple timeframes before committing to a move. The more timeframes that align, the stronger the move. |
| **Plumbing cross-ref** | hierarchy Part 4.4 (Trading Desk Layers). Institutional decision-making happens across multiple time horizons. |
| **What to look for** | Same level = resistance on daily, 4H, AND 1H. When price sweeps that level cleanly across all three, the reversal has higher conviction. |
| **Entry rules** | 1. Identify a key level that shows on 3+ timeframes. 2. Wait for price to sweep it on the lowest timeframe (1H). 3. Confirm with CHoCH on the 5-min. 4. Enter. |
| **Proof to advance** | 3 trades with 3+ timeframe confluence. |

**Week 11 — FORWARD TESTING (Live application, full system)**

| Element | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Goal** | NOT to make money. To run the full system end-to-end for 20 trades. |
| **What to do** | Every session: workflow → bridge → gangotri protocol → playbook → journal. No shortcuts. |
| **Entry rules** | Apply all 10 weeks of concepts. No new patterns. Focus on: is the system actually executable? Where does it break? |
| **Review focus** | 1. Did I follow the 3-question gate every time? 2. Which patterns had the most setups? 3. Which patterns had the best win rate? 4. Where did the system fail me? |
| **Proof to advance** | 20 journaled trades. Win rate, avg R:R, and a 1-page system critique written. |

**Week 12 — EVAL PREP (Transition to prop firm challenge)**

| Element | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Goal** | Simulate eval conditions. Train yourself to PASS, not to make money. |
| **What to do** | Read: `trading/eval-mode-protocol.md`. Switch to eval-mode sizing. Run 10 sessions with eval rules. |
| **Entry rules** | Max 1-2 trades per day. Max 0.25-0.5% risk per trade. Hard stop at 1% daily loss. |
| **Review focus** | 1. Did I breach any eval limit? 2. Did I treat it differently knowing it was "eval"? 3. Am I ready to buy the first eval? |
| **Proof to advance** | 10 eval-mode sessions with zero max-drawdown breaches. Ready to fund an evaluation. |

## THE RULES

1. **Do not advance until proof is met.** The proof gates exist because each concept builds on the last. Skip a foundation, and Month 2 patterns won't work.
2. **If you repeat a week, change your approach.** Same concept, different lens. If sweeps didn't work with the plumbing signal you were watching, try a different signal from the daily workflow. The concept isn't wrong — your application is.
3. **Journal every trade.** Even skipped trades. "I saw a sweep but the plumbing said contraction. I skipped. Price reversed." That's a WIN — you followed the system.
4. **When the tracker fills up, you're ready.** 12 weeks of structured learning, ~120 journaled trades, eval-ready risk habits. That's the gate. Not 6 months of demo. 12 focused weeks.
5. **The syllabus is not the teacher. The screen is.** Every line in this file is a suggestion. The only real curriculum is price. Let the chart tell you what you need to study next.
