# [Futures](09-futures-trading-energy-and-receipts.md) Trading: Energy and Receipts

## Why [Futures](09-futures-trading-energy-and-receipts.md) After [Forex](08-forex-trading-the-purest-flow.md)

[Forex](08-forex-trading-the-purest-flow.md) teaches you how NATIONS' receipts move against each other. [Futures](09-futures-trading-energy-and-receipts.md) teaches you how the ACTUAL ENERGY underneath those receipts is priced.

Oil. Gas. Gold. Wheat. Copper. These are the raw inputs to human civilization. They are what [money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md) is a receipt FOR. When you trade [futures](09-futures-trading-energy-and-receipts.md), you are trading the thing itself — not the receipt for the thing, but the energy, the commodity, the raw material.

**This is the deepest layer of the game.** Everything above — stocks, currencies, bonds — is an abstraction of what happens here.

## I. What a [Futures](09-futures-trading-energy-and-receipts.md) Contract ACTUALLY Is

A [futures](09-futures-trading-energy-and-receipts.md) contract = an agreement to buy or sell a specific quantity of a commodity at a specific price on a specific date in the future.

But strip the textbook language. Here's what it really is:

**A [futures](09-futures-trading-energy-and-receipts.md) contract is a BET on the future price of ENERGY.**

- Crude oil [futures](09-futures-trading-energy-and-receipts.md) = bet on how much energy will cost in 3 months
- Natural gas [futures](09-futures-trading-energy-and-receipts.md) = bet on how much heating/industry will cost next winter
- Gold [futures](09-futures-trading-energy-and-receipts.md) = bet on how much people will distrust paper receipts in the future
- Wheat [futures](09-futures-trading-energy-and-receipts.md) = bet on how much food will cost at harvest
- S&P 500 [futures](09-futures-trading-energy-and-receipts.md) (ES) = bet on how much corporate energy output will be worth

The [futures](09-futures-trading-energy-and-receipts.md) market is where the REAL economy meets the FINANCIAL economy. Producers (farmers, miners, drillers) use it to lock in prices. Speculators use it to bet on direction. The tension between these two groups is where the [money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md) is.

## III. The Esoteric Structure of [Futures](09-futures-trading-energy-and-receipts.md) Markets

### Contango and Backwardation — The Hidden Tax and Hidden Gift

This is what most retail [futures](09-futures-trading-energy-and-receipts.md) traders never learn, and it's the most important structural feature:

**Contango** = [futures](09-futures-trading-energy-and-receipts.md) price > current spot price  
**Backwardation** = [futures](09-futures-trading-energy-and-receipts.md) price < current spot price

| Structure | What It Means | Who Benefits | Why |
|-----------|--------------|--------------|-----|
| **Contango** | Future price is higher than today's price | Long ETF holders GET CRUSHED. Producers benefit. | Storing commodities costs [money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md) (storage, insurance, financing). The [futures](09-futures-trading-energy-and-receipts.md) price bakes in these costs. If you hold a long [futures](09-futures-trading-energy-and-receipts.md) ETF in contango, you pay this "roll cost" every month when the contract expires and you must roll to the next (more expensive) month. It's a slow bleed. |
| **Backwardation** | Future price is lower than today's price | Long holders GET PAID to roll. Consumers benefit. | Supply is tight NOW. People will pay a premium for immediate delivery. When you roll your contract forward, you BUY at a LOWER price. This is positive carry — free [money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md) for being long. |

**Hard rule:** NEVER hold a commodity ETF long-term without checking contango/backwardation. USO (oil ETF) lost 90%+ of its value over a decade not because oil went to zero, but because contango ate the returns through roll costs. You could have been RIGHT about oil direction and STILL lost [money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md).

### The COT Report — See Where the Smart [Money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md) Is

The Commitments of Traders report (COT) is published weekly by the CFTC. It shows you the positioning of three groups:

| Group | Who They Are | What Their Positioning Means |
|-------|-------------|------------------------------|
| **Commercials (Hedgers)** | Producers and consumers of the actual commodity — oil companies, farmers, miners | They hedge. When they are EXTREMELY long or short, it signals they see a price extreme. They are the "smart [money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md)" for the long term. |
| **Non-Commercials (Large Speculators)** | Hedge funds, CTAs, large traders | They trend-follow. When they are heavily one-sided, the trend is mature. Reversal risk is high. |
| **Non-Reportable (Small Speculators)** | Retail | They are usually wrong at extremes. When small specs are maximally long → top is near. When maximally short → bottom is near. |

**How to use it:**
- Look for DIVERGENCES between commercials and speculators
- When commercials are heavily long and speculators heavily short → bottom is near (smart [money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md) loading)
- When commercials are heavily short and speculators heavily long → top is near (smart [money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md) distributing)
- The COT is a WEEKLY indicator — use it for swing trades and position trades, not day trades

**Where to find it:** cftc.gov — published every Friday at 3:30 PM ET with data as of Tuesday

## IV. The Energy Markets — Oil and Gas for a Canadian Trader

### WTI Crude Oil (CL)

This is your home turf. Canada produces oil. The Canadian dollar breathes oil. Here's how to trade it:

**The inventory cycle:**

| Report | When | How to Trade It |
|--------|------|-----------------|
| **API Weekly Inventory** | Tuesday 4:30 PM ET | Private survey. Gives a preview. Sometimes moves markets, sometimes doesn't. |
| **EIA Weekly Inventory** | Wednesday 10:30 AM ET | THE number. Build = bearish. Draw = bullish. But... |
| **Refinery utilization** | In the EIA report | High refinery runs = demand is strong even if inventory builds. Context matters. |
| **Cushed storage** | In the EIA report | Cushing, OK is the delivery point for WTI. Low Cushing storage = VERY bullish (squeezes possible). |

**The seasonal pattern:**

| Season | Direction | Why |
|--------|-----------|-----|
| **Spring (Feb–Apr)** | Often weak | Refinery maintenance. Less demand. |
| **Summer (May–Aug)** | Often strong | Driving season. Gasoline demand peaks. |
| **Fall (Sep–Oct)** | Often weak | Seasonal demand drop. Refinery switch to winter blend. |
| **Winter (Nov–Jan)** | Mixed | Heating demand supports. But overall demand drops. |

**Hard rule:** Trade the inventory report like a [forex](08-forex-trading-the-purest-flow.md) news release — wait for the initial spike, then fade it or follow it based on the context (Cushing, refinery runs, products). Never enter in the first 30 seconds.

### Natural Gas (NG)

Natural gas is the most volatile commodity future. It can move 5-10% in a single day. Respect it or it will destroy you.

**The key driver: WEATHER.**

- Cold winter forecast → NG rallies (heating demand)
- Warm winter → NG crashes (oversupply, low demand)
- Hot summer → NG rallies (air conditioning = electricity = gas-fired power plants)
- Hurricane in Gulf → NG spikes (production shut-ins)

**How to trade it:**
- Follow the weather models (GFS, ECMWF) — they update twice daily
- Watch the storage report (Thursday 10:30 AM ET) — same as oil inventory but for gas
- Natural gas is a WINTER play. The classic trade: buy NG in September before heating season, sell in January when demand peaks. This is not guaranteed every year, but it works more often than not.

**Hard rule:** Size SMALL on natural gas. A 5% move against you with full leverage = account death. Use 50% of your normal position size for NG. It is deservedly called the " Widowmaker."

## V. Gold — The Anti-Receipt

Gold is the opposite of fiat [money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md). It is the receipt that NO ONE issued. It exists outside the [bank](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md)ing system. It cannot be created by a [central [bank](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md)](05-how-[money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md)-flows-in-the-system.md) keystroke. It must be dug out of the ground at real cost.

**Gold is the market's measure of distrust in paper receipts.**

| When Gold Goes Up | What It Means |
|-------------------|---------------|
| Real [interest rate](08-forex-trading-the-purest-flow.md)s falling (nominal rates minus [inflation](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md)) | Holding paper earns less than nothing after [inflation](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md) → gold becomes attractive |
| USD weakening | The world's reserve receipt is losing value → people want the non-receipt |
| [Central [bank](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md)](05-how-[money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md)-flows-in-the-system.md)s buying | Sovereigns are hedging against the system they run → the ultimate signal |
| Geopolitical crisis | Trust in institutions drops → gold rises as the trust-less asset |
| [Bank](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md)ing crisis | If [bank](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md)s fail, receipts might be worthless → gold is the alternative |

| When Gold Goes Down | What It Means |
|---------------------|---------------|
| Real [interest rate](08-forex-trading-the-purest-flow.md)s rising | Paper yields more than [inflation](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md) → gold pays no yield, so it loses appeal |
| USD strengthening | Reserve currency strong → less need for alternative |
| Risk-on environment | People want growth assets (stocks), not insurance |
| [Central [bank](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md)](05-how-[money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md)-flows-in-the-system.md) selling | Sovereigns trust the system → why would you? |

**Canadian edge:** Canada mines gold. The TSX is loaded with gold miners. If you're trading gold [futures](09-futures-trading-energy-and-receipts.md), you can also look at gold miner stocks (Barrick, Agnico Eagle, etc.) for confirmation signals. When miners lead gold → gold is about to move. When gold leads miners → miners are catching up.

## VI. The [Futures](09-futures-trading-energy-and-receipts.md)-Specific Hard Rules

1. **Respect margin.** [Futures](09-futures-trading-energy-and-receipts.md) leverage is enormous. One CL contract = 1,000 barrels = ~$70,000 notional with only ~$5,000-$10,000 margin. A 1% move = $700. That's 7-14% of your margin per 1% move. Size accordingly.

2. **Always know the contract month you're trading.** Volume shifts to the next month near expiration. Don't get stuck in an illiquid month.

3. **Check contango/backwardation before holding ANY position overnight.** If you're in contango, the roll cost is eroding your P&L silently.

4. **Check the COT report weekly.** It tells you where commercials (smart [money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md)) vs. speculators (momentum) are positioned.

5. **Trade the EIA/API with the same news-fade discipline as [forex](08-forex-trading-the-purest-flow.md).** Wait for the spike, then decide.

6. **Natural gas = 50% of normal size. Always.** No exceptions. The Widowmaker has killed careers.

7. **Gold is a POSITION trade, not a day trade.** It moves on real [interest rate](08-forex-trading-the-purest-flow.md)s and [central [bank](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md)](05-how-[money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md)-flows-in-the-system.md) policy. These change over weeks and months, not minutes. Trade gold on 4H/daily charts, not 1M/5M.

8. **Know the delivery months for each contract.** Crude oil: every month. Natural gas: every month. Gold: Feb, Apr, Jun, Aug, Dec. Wheat: Mar, May, Jul, Sep, Dec.

9. **The Canadian dollar is your embedded oil trade.** If you're long oil AND long USDCAD (short CAD), you're making the same bet twice. Know your embedded positions.

10. **The trend in [futures](09-futures-trading-energy-and-receipts.md) is YOUR friend until the physical market disagrees.** Technical analysis works until supply/demand fundamentals override it. Always check the physical market (inventories, production, demand) before taking a large technical trade.

## VII. The Connection: [Forex](08-forex-trading-the-purest-flow.md) + [Futures](09-futures-trading-energy-and-receipts.md) = The Full Picture

[Forex](08-forex-trading-the-purest-flow.md) tells you which receipt (currency) the market prefers. [Futures](09-futures-trading-energy-and-receipts.md) tell you what the RECEIPTS ARE PRICING. Together, they give you the complete view:

- Oil rising + CAD strengthening = the market is pricing energy scarcity and rewarding energy producers → trade with this until the signal changes
- Oil rising + CAD weakening = something is wrong with Canada specifically (political risk, pipeline issues, housing crisis) → fade CAD, stay long oil
- Gold rising + USD rising = the market is scared of SOMETHING beyond normal USD dynamics (systemic risk, [bank](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md)ing crisis) → get defensive

**The matrix:**

| | Oil Rising | Oil Falling |
|---|---|---|
| **USD Rising** | Stagflation risk — costs up, growth uncertain. Gold should rise too. | Deflation — USD king, commodities crushed. Long USD, short commodities. |
| **USD Falling** | Classic reflation — everything up. Long risk, long CAD/AUD, long commodities. | Demand collapse — recession. Long USD, short equities, watch gold. |
