## I. The Monetary Plumbing: Where to Look *This Week*
Forget lagging indicators (MACD, RSI). Watch these **real-time pressure points**—updated weekly. These show where *new* liquidity is being injected or drained.

| **Pressure Point** | **What It Measures** | **Where to See It (Free Data)** | **What It Means for Flow** |
|--------------------|----------------------|---------------------------------|----------------------------|
| **BoC Balance Sheet Assets** | Total Bank of Canada assets (liquidity injected via QE, repos, etc.) | [BoC Weekly Assets](https://www.bankofcanada.ca/rates/financial-market-indicators/balance-sheet/) | 📈 Rising = new liquidity entering system → look for where it flows first (often CAD pairs, commodities, bank stocks).<br>📉 Falling = liquidity draining → defensive posture (USD, bonds, gold). |
| **BoC Net Repo Position** | BoC’s net liquidity injection/withdrawal via repos (reverse repos = draining) | [BoC Market Operations](https://www.bankofcanada.ca/rates/interest-rates/corporate-bond-yields/) → "Repo Operations" | 📈 Positive (net repo) = BoC *adding* liquidity → risk-on.<br>📉 Negative (net reverse repo) = BoC *removing* liquidity → risk-off. |
| **Charter Bank Credit (CMBSC)** | Total credit extended by Canadian chartered banks (loans + mortgages + biz credit) | [Stats Canada Table 10-10-0005-01](https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/t1/tbl1/en/tv.action?pid=1010000501) (monthly, but trend is key) | 📈 Rising = banks creating new money via loans → broad economic activity.<br>📉 Falling = credit crunch → recession risk. |
| **Government of Canada Bond Auctions (Bid-to-Cover)** | Demand for new debt issuance | [GoC Auction Results](https://www.canada.ca/en/department-finance/services/government-securities-debt-auctions/auction-results.html) | 📉 Low bid-to-cover (<2.0) = weak demand → yields may rise despite BoC holding rates (crowding-out risk).<br>📈 High bid-to-cover (>2.5) = strong demand → yields stable/falling. |
| **WTI Crude Oil Inventory Change** | Weekly US oil storage build/draw (proxy for global demand/supply shock) | [EIA Weekly Petroleum Status Report](https://www.eia.gov/petroleum/supply/weekly/) (Wed 10:30 AM ET) | 📉 Draw (drawdown) = bullish for oil → bullish for CAD (petro-currency).<br>📈 Build = bearish for oil → bearish for CAD.<br>⚠️ **Key for USD/CAD**: Trade the *fade* of the initial spike (see 08-forex-trading-the-purest-flow.md#the-news-fade). |
| **CAD Oil Correlation (30-day)** | How tightly CAD moves with WTI oil | [TradingView CAD/WTI chart](https://www.tradingview.com/chart/?symbol=FX_IDC%3ACAD) → compare to CL1! | 📈 Rising correlation = oil is *the* driver of CAD (trade oil → CAD).<br>📉 Falling correlation = other forces (rates, risk) dominating CAD → check BoC/Fed divergence. |
| **US Treasury TGA (Treasury General Account)** | Cash balance at the Fed (when high, Treasury is *not* spending → net liquidity drain; when low, Treasury is spending → net liquidity inject) | [FRED TGA Balance](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/WTSLA) | 📈 Rising TGA = Treasury hoarding cash → net liquidity *drain* from system.<br>📉 Falling TGA = Treasury spending → net liquidity *inject* (often bullish for risk assets). |
| **CFTC COT Report: Non-Commercial (Speculator) Positioning** | Where leveraged funds are betting (futures markets) | [CFTC COT Futures](https://www.cftc.gov/MarketReports/CommitmentsofTraders/index.htm) (every Friday 3:30 PM ET) | 📈 Extreme long = crowded trade → vulnerable to reversal.<br>📈 Extreme short = oversold → potential squeeze.<br>🔑 **Key**: Look for *divergence* vs. commercials (hedgers). When specs are long and commercials are short → top forming. |

## II. Where Does the Flow *Actually* Go? (The Transmission Mechanism)
New liquidity doesn’t go everywhere at once. It follows a **sequence**—like water finding the path of least resistance. Know the sequence, and you know where to tap.

### Phase 1: Immediate Reaction (0-72 Hours)
- **Where it lands first**: **Bank reserves**, **short-term govt bills**, **USD/JPY** (carry funding currency), **gold** (if real rates fall).
- **What to trade**: 
  - Short-term: **USD/JPY** (if BoC/Fed divergence), **2Y bond yields**, **gold** (if real rates drop).
  - Example: BoC cuts rates while Fed holds → CAD weakness → short USD/CAD, long gold (if real rates fall).

### Phase 2: Early Transmission (1-4 Weeks)
- **Where it flows next**: **Corporate bonds**, **EM equities/commodities**, **CAD (via oil)**, **high-yield bonds**.
- **What to trade**:
  - CAD pairs (USD/CAD, CAD/JPY) if oil-driven.
  - Commodity futures (CL, NG, GC) if reflation signal.
  - CDN bank stocks (RY, TD, BMO) if credit expansion expected.
- **Example**: EIA crude draw + BoC holding = long CAD via oil beta (short USD/CAD, long WTI).

### Phase 3: Late Transmission (1-6 Months)
- **Where it flows last**: **Equities (S&P 500, TSX)**, **real estate**, **long-term bonds** (if inflation fears fade).
- **What to trade**:
  - Equity indices (VTI, VFV, XIC) for broad exposure.
  - Long bonds (ZB, CGB) only if *real* rates are falling (not just nominal).
  - **Caution**: This is where retail piled in 2021—often the *worst* time to enter.

> 🎯 **Your Rule**:  
> **Tap in Phase 1 or 2.**  
> By Phase 3, the flow is visible to everyone—returns are low, and reversal risk is high.  
> The 1% gets in when the flow is *starting*, not when it’s a flood.
