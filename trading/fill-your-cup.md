## THE THREE PHASES

```
PHASE 1                PHASE 2                 PHASE 3
PROVE IT              SCALE IT                LIVE IT
────────              ───────                 ───────
First eval pass       Multiple funded         Cash flow exceeds
→ First payout        accounts running        expenses → freedom
→ Second eval         → Compounding           → Portfolio income
→ Third eval          → Reinvestment          → Time sovereignty
```

Each phase feeds the next. Do not skip. Do not rush. The compound curve is exponential — the first 80% of the curve happens in the last 20% of time.

## PHASE 1 — PROVE IT (Month 1-3)

### Milestones

| Milestone | Target | How |
|-----------|--------|-----|
| First eval pass | Month 1-2 | Goat Funded Trader, $5K or $10K account, 1 MES, 0.25% risk |
| First payout | Month 2-3 | After passing, first monthly payout request |
| Second eval pass | Month 2-3 | Second Goat account or FTMO challenge |
| Third eval pass | Month 3 | Third funded account running |
| Total funded capital | $15K-$30K | 3 × $5K-$10K accounts |

### Cash Flow in Phase 1

| Account Size | Monthly Target (10% / conservative) | Your Share (80-100%) | Monthly Take-Home |
|-------------|-----------------------------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| $5,000 | $500 | $400-500 | $400-500 |
| $10,000 | $1,000 | $800-1,000 | $800-1,000 |
| 3 × $5K | $1,500 | $1,200-1,500 | $1,200-1,500 |
| 3 × $10K | $3,000 | $2,400-3,000 | $2,400-3,000 |

**Phase 1 payout rule:** Take 100% of first payout. You've earned it. This validates the system. Then reinvest 50% of subsequent payouts into more evals.

### First Payout — What to Do

1. Receive the money — let yourself feel it
2. Put 50% into your emergency buffer
3. Use 25% to fund the next eval
4. Keep 25% as a reward (you need the dopamine to continue)
5. Write in your journal: "I made ___ from trading this month. The system works."

### The Scaling Rules

| Account Size | How to Scale | Risk |
|-------------|-------------|------|
| $10K | 1 MES per trade, 0.5% risk | Low |
| $25K | 1-2 MES or 1 MNQ, 0.5% risk | Low-Medium |
| $50K | 2-3 MES or 2 MNQ, 0.5% risk | Medium |
| $100K | 3-4 MES, or 2 MES + 2 MNQ, correlated | Medium-High |

**Scale only when:** You've had 3 consecutive profitable months at the current level. If you're making $2K/month on $30K, don't jump to $50K until you prove you're ready.

### Account Hierarchy

Don't put all your risk in one firm:

| Tier | Firm | % of Total Capital | Purpose |
|------|------|-------------------|---------|
| Core | Goat Funded Trader | 50% | Primary income — easiest rules, no time limit |
| Growth | FTMO | 30% | Secondary income — reputation, larger caps |
| Experiment | Apex or Topstep | 20% | Test new strategies, scale if they work |

**Never have more than 50% of your total funded capital at a single firm.** Diversification applies to prop firms too.

**Reality check:** Phase 3 is a multi-year journey. The goal is not to get there fast. The goal is to stay alive long enough to get there.

### The 3 Bucket System

Once your monthly trading income exceeds your expenses:

| Bucket | Allocation | Purpose |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| **Living** | 30% | Monthly expenses, lifestyle, freedom |
| **Reinvestment** | 30% | More evals, larger accounts, new instruments |
| **Wealth** | 40% | Long-term: index funds, real estate, alternative assets |

**The wealth bucket rules:**
- Once it reaches $100K, move it to a separate brokerage account
- Invest 70% in broad market index funds (VTI, VXUS, BND)
- Keep 20% in cash (opportunity fund)
- Use 10% for alternative assets (real estate syndication, private credit)
- Never trade the wealth bucket. It's your backup. Your "fuck you" fund.

## THE ANTI-FRAGILE RULES

### Rule 1: Withdraw Your Original Investment First

Once your funded accounts have produced $X in payouts (where X = what you spent on evals + setup), you're playing with house money.

| Cost | Recoup Point |
|------|-------------|
| First eval ($35-120) | First payout covers it |
| All Year 1 evals (~$500) | Month 3 payout covers it |
| Software, data, platform fees (~$100/mo) | Included in monthly costs |

**After recoup:** You are trading with zero personal financial risk. Every dollar earned is profit. This changes your psychology completely.

### Rule 2: The 3-Month Emergency Fund

Before scaling beyond $125K in funded capital, build a personal emergency fund of 3 months' expenses in cash.

| Monthly Expenses | Emergency Fund Target |
|----------------|---------------------|
| $2,000 | $6,000 |
| $3,000 | $9,000 |
| $5,000 | $15,000 |

**This is the line between "trader" and "gambler."** With an emergency fund, you can take a month off without stress. Without it, every losing day is a crisis.

### Rule 3: The 20% Max Drawdown Rule

If your funded capital drops 20% from its peak (across ALL accounts combined):
1. Stop trading for 1 week
2. Reduce all positions by 50%
3. Identify what changed in the market regime
4. Only resume after plumbing data confirms the regime

**The 20% drawdown is not a loss. It's a signal.** The signal says: "Something has changed that your current strategy is not adapted to." Adapt first, then trade.

### Rule 4: Never Reinvest 100% Into Trading

The biggest mistake funded traders make: they take every payout and buy more evals. This creates concentration risk.

| Income Level | Max % to Reinvest | Why |
|-------------|------------------|-----|
| < $5K/mo | 50% | Need to build emergency fund first |
| $5-10K/mo | 40% | Emergency fund done, start wealth bucket |
| $10-25K/mo | 30% | Wealth bucket dominates |
| $25K+/mo | 20% | Preserve capital, diversify |
