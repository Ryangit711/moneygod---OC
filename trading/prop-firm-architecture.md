## THE RIGHT PLATFORM TO MASTER — Answering Your Question

### The Order of Mastery

```
PHASE 1 (NOW): TradingView
├── Learn: chart reading, indicators, drawing tools, alerts
├── Practice: paper trading on TradingView
├── Time: 2-4 weeks
└── This is your ANALYSIS layer — used for EVERYTHING, forever

PHASE 2 (WEEK 1-12): MT5
├── Learn: order placement, stop loss, take profit, lot sizing
├── Practice: MT5 demo from FTMO (free 14-day) or Goat Funded ($17)
├── Time: 12 weeks (syllabus)
└── This is your FX EXECUTION layer — used for FX prop firms

PHASE 3 (MONTH 4+): Tradovate
├── Learn: futures contracts, tick values, margin, DOM (basic)
├── Practice: Tradovate paper trading (free)
├── Time: 2-4 weeks
└── This is your FUTURES EXECUTION layer — used for futures prop firms

PHASE 4 (YEAR 1+): TWS (Interactive Brokers)
├── Learn: complex orders, multi-asset, real market microstructure
├── Practice: IBKR paper account (free, most realistic)
├── Time: ongoing
└── This is your LONG-TERM HOME — self-directed with real capital
```

### Why This Order

| Platform | When | Why |
|----------|------|-----|
| TradingView first | Always | Best charting UX. You'll use this forever regardless of which firm you trade with. |
| MT5 second | Months 1-3 | 80% of FX prop firms use MT5. Learn it once, use it everywhere. |
| Tradovate third | Month 4+ | Cleanest futures platform. Web-based. Most futures firms support it. |
| TWS last | Year 1+ | Most complex but most powerful. Only after you're funded and scaling. |

### What NOT to Master (Yet)

| Platform | Why Skip for Now |
|----------|-----------------|
| NinjaTrader | Powerful but desktop-heavy, steep learning curve. Use Tradovate first (same firms support both). |
| Rithmic | Backend data feed, not a trading interface. You don't interact with it directly. |
| cTrader | Good platform but fewer firms use it. MT5 is more universal. |
| Sierra Chart | Ultra-advanced, institutional-grade. Overkill for your current level. |
| Quantower | Advanced order flow. Learn after you're consistently profitable. |

### CRA Tax Implications

| Income Type | Tax Treatment |
|-------------|--------------|
| Prop firm profit split | Taxable at marginal rate |
| Evaluation fee | Business expense (deductible) |
| Platform/data costs | Business expense (deductible) |
| Home office | Deductible (if applicable) |

**Critical:** Do NOT trade prop firm accounts inside TFSA/RRSP. CRA treats active trading as business income, not capital gains. Use a margin account or hold prop firm accounts outside registered accounts.

## THE SCALING ARCHITECTURE — From 1 Account to Many

### Phase 1: Prove It (1 Account)

```
GOAL: Pass 1 eval, get funded, take first payout
FIRM: FTMO (forex) or MyFundedFutures (futures)
ACCOUNT SIZE: $10K-$25K
RISK: 0.25% per trade
TIMELINE: Month 1-3
```

### Phase 2: Duplicate It (2-3 Accounts)

```
GOAL: Prove consistency across multiple accounts
FIRM: Same firm, or add a second firm
ACCOUNT SIZE: 2-3 × $10K-$25K
RISK: 0.25% per trade per account
TIMELINE: Month 3-6
RULE: If account 1 is in drawdown, DO NOT open account 2
```

### Phase 3: Diversify It (3-5 Accounts, 2-3 Firms)

```
GOAL: Spread counterparty risk across firms
FIRMS: 1 FX firm (FTMO) + 1 futures firm (MyFundedFutures) + 1 backup
ACCOUNT SIZE: 3-5 × $10K-$50K
RISK: 0.25% per trade per account, max 3% combined daily loss
TIMELINE: Month 6-12
RULE: Never have >50% of capital at one firm
```

### Phase 4: Compound It (5+ Accounts)

```
GOAL: Scale funded capital to $100K+
FIRMS: 3-4 firms, mix of FX and futures
ACCOUNT SIZE: 5-10 accounts, $100K+ total
RISK: 0.25-0.5% per trade per account
TIMELINE: Year 1-2
RULE: Scale only after 3 consecutive profitable months
```

## THE ONE-STOP GATEWAY ANSWER

You asked about trading multiple prop accounts via 1 common gateway. Here's the truth:

**There is no single platform that connects to all prop firms.** Each firm gives you separate credentials on their platform.

**But you CAN create a unified execution flow:**

```
LAYER 1: TradingView (1 window, analysis for ALL accounts)
├── Open once, mark up charts, set alerts
├── This is your brain — same analysis regardless of which firm you're trading

LAYER 2: Per-Firm Execution (separate windows per firm)
├── MT5 window for FTMO (FX)
├── Tradovate window for MyFundedFutures (futures)
├── Each has its own login, its own rules, its own drawdown

LAYER 3: Single Risk Model (applied uniformly)
├── 0.25% per trade, per account
├── 2% daily max, per account
├── 3% combined daily max across ALL accounts
├── If 2 accounts hit daily loss → stop ALL accounts

LAYER 4: Single Journal (tracks everything)
├── systems/trade-journal-template.md
├── One journal entry per trade, noting which firm/account
├── Weekly review: combined P&L across all accounts
```

**The gateway is not a piece of software. It's a protocol.** The same analysis, the same risk rules, the same journal — applied uniformly across every account you trade.
