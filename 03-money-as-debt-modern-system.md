# [Money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md) as [Debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md): The Modern System

## How Modern [Money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md) Actually Works

Most people think [money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md) works like this: you work, you get paid, you deposit it in a [bank](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md), the [bank](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md) lends your deposit to someone else. **Wrong.**

Here's how it actually works: when a [bank](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md) issues a loan, it doesn't lend out someone else's deposit. It **creates new [money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md) by typing a number into an account**. That number is [debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md). **Over 90% of [money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md) in circulation today is created this way — as [bank](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md) loans.**

---

## The [Bank](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md) [Money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md) Creation Process

1. A borrower walks into a [bank](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md) and asks for a loan
2. The [bank](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md) checks their [credit](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md)worthiness
3. If approved, the [bank](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md) **creates a deposit** in the borrower's account out of nothing
4. The borrower now has "[money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md)" to spend
5. That [money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md) didn't exist before the loan was made
6. The loan is an **asset** on the [bank](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md)'s balance sheet; the deposit is a **liability**
7. The borrower must repay the principal **plus interest**

The key insight: **the interest was never created.** Only the principal is created by the loan. So where does the [money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md) to pay interest come from? From someone else's loan. Which means the system requires **constant growth in [debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)** just to function.

---

## [Debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md) in Modern Economies

Today **most [money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md) is [debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)**:

| Form | How It Works |
|------|--------------|
| **[Bank](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md) deposits** | When you deposit cash, the [bank](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md) records a liability ("we owe you X"). That liability is used for loans, payments, etc. |
| **Government bonds** | Sovereign [debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md) is a promise to repay with interest; these bonds are traded like assets |
| **[Credit](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md) cards & loans** | Direct consumer [debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md), recorded electronically |

The monetary system is essentially a web of promises — **IOUs** — that circulate as "[money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md)."

---

## Why the System Requires Growth

Because interest must be paid on every loan, but only principal is created:

- Total [debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md) in the system = sum of all principals + sum of all interest owed
- Total [money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md) in the system = sum of all principals only
- The gap = interest that must be earned by competing for the existing [money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md) supply
- This means someone, somewhere, always defaults — or new loans must be issued to keep the system liquid

This is why there **must** be constant economic growth in a [debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-based monetary system. Not because growth is inherently good. Because without it, the math collapses. Defaults cascade. [Bank](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md)s fail. Recessions and depressions follow.

## [Inflation](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md) as Hidden Tax

[Inflation](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md) is not just "prices going up." It is a **hidden transfer of wealth**:

- From **savers** (whose [money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md) loses purchasing power) → to **[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)ors** (whose [debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)s shrink in real terms)
- The biggest [debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)or in any country = **the government**
- When the government prints [money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md) or the [central [bank](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md)](05-how-[money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md)-flows-in-the-system.md) expands the [money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md) supply, it is effectively taxing savers to pay down its own real [debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)
- This is why [inflation](03-money-as-debt-modern-system.md) is called "the invisible tax"

## The Fundamental Design

[Money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md) and [debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md) are **engineered systems of obligation**:

- **[Money](03-money-as-[debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)-modern-system.md)** = a token of claim on other people's labor and resources
- **[Debt](01-origins-of-money-and-debt.md)** = a promise enforced by power (legal, military, social)
- Both exist because humans needed to track obligations at scale
- They were **never neutral** — from Sumer and Babylon to Wall Street, they are tools of power
- The question is always: **who creates the obligations, and who is forced to fulfill them?**
